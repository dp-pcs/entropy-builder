# Company Rules — ESW Operating Model

> **MANDATORY PROCESSING:** This document must be read and applied before generating any customer-specific playbook, renewal scenario, or pricing recommendation. Every scenario modeled must operate within these constraints. No exceptions unless explicitly noted.

---

## 1. Pricing Structure

### The Model

Standardized, non-negotiable pricing driven by our investment in products to ensure stability, performance, and long-term viability.

**WE DO NOT DEVIATE FROM THE MODEL.**

### Pricing Tiers

| Success Level | Price Increase at Renewal |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| **Standard Success** | **25%** per renewal |
| **Platinum Success** | **45%** per renewal |

### Key Pricing Principles

- **No discounts.** Ever. No volume discounts, no loyalty discounts, no competitive discounts.
- **No legacy pricing.** Customers do not retain old pricing regardless of tenure.
- **No downsell price reduction.** If a customer reduces licenses (e.g., 100 → 80), their price remains unchanged. The 25% or 45% increase still applies to the full contract value. Advise customers against reducing licenses since price remains the same.
- **Pricing ensures product sustainability.** This is the rationale — our continued investment in stability, performance, and long-term viability of the product requires this model.

### Compounding Effect (Critical for Scenario Modeling)

Price increases compound at every renewal cycle:

**Standard Success (25%) — 1-Year Contract Example:**
| Year | Price (starting at $100K) |
|------|--------------------------|
| Year 1 | $100,000 |
| Year 2 | $125,000 |
| Year 3 | $156,250 |
| Year 4 | $195,313 |
| Year 5 | $244,141 |

**Platinum Success (45%) — 1-Year Contract Example:**
| Year | Price (starting at $100K) |
|------|--------------------------|
| Year 1 | $100,000 |
| Year 2 | $145,000 |
| Year 3 | $210,250 |
| Year 4 | $304,863 |
| Year 5 | $442,050 |

> **Playbook Implication:** When modeling renewal scenarios, always calculate the compounded price. Never present the increase as a one-time event — show the customer's trajectory so they understand the value of multi-year contracts.

## 2. Contract Terms

### Available Contract Lengths

| Term | Pricing Behavior |
|------|-----------------|
| **1-Year** | Subject to price increase at every annual renewal |
| **3-Year** | Price locked for three years (no increases during term) |
| **5-Year** | Price locked for five years (no increases during term) |

**No other contract durations are available.** Customers cannot sign for 2 years, 4 years, or any other term.

### Multi-Year Contract Incentive

There are **no discounts** for signing multi-year contracts. The sole incentive is **price protection** — locking in the current rate and avoiding compounding increases.

**Example — Standard Success, current price $125K:**
| Option | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 | 3-Year Total |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|-------------|
| 1-Year renewals | $125K | $156K | $195K | **$476K** |
| 3-Year contract | $125K | $125K | $125K | **$375K** |
| **Savings** | — | — | — | **$101K (21%)** |

> **Playbook Implication:** The multi-year value proposition is the primary tool for customer retention. Always model the compounding cost comparison when presenting contract options.

### Auto-Renewal Clause

Auto-renewal is a **standard, non-negotiable** component of all contracts. It is part of our company policy to mitigate risk and ensure consistent service delivery. This is not optional and cannot be removed.

### Contract Redlining Rules

| Customer ARR | Redline Policy |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Under $100K/year** | Contract cannot be redlined or modified. Standardized, non-negotiable document. |
| **Over $100K/year** | Reasonable modifications may be considered, subject to legal team review. |

**Critical Rule:** We do **NOT** proactively disclose that redlining is possible for >$100K customers. The standard communication is: *"The contract is a standardized, non-negotiable document, part of our company policy to mitigate risk and ensure consistent service delivery."*

If and only if a customer **actively requests** redlining and pays >$100K, we may engage legal. However, legal engagement is expensive and should be minimized. Do not encourage or suggest redlining — only respond if the customer raises it.

> **Playbook Implication:** Never include "offer contract modifications" as a negotiation lever in any playbook. If a playbook addresses a >$100K customer who is likely to push back on terms, note that redlining is a *reactive* option only — never a proactive offer.

## 3. Revenue Opportunities

### During Contract Term

There are only **three** ways to generate additional revenue from an existing customer during their contract:

| Opportunity | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Professional Services** | Custom integrations to solve identified product pain points. Engage the PS team when specific technical issues are surfaced. |
| **Upsell — Additional Licenses** | Prorated cost based on current contract value per license per day. Strategy: price one license higher, then offer a bundle at lower per-license cost to incentivize purchasing more. |
| **Upsell — Standard → Platinum** | Move from 25% to 45% success tier. Historically low success rate due to perceived lack of value in Platinum delivery. *Currently being reworked — update this section when new Platinum entitlements are finalized.* |

### At Renewal

| Opportunity | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Retention via Multi-Year Extension** | Convert 1-year contracts to 3-year or 5-year. **Must be initiated >180 days before renewal date.** If ≤180 days, the customer must go through the standard renewal process. |
| **Standard Renewal** | Apply the 25% or 45% price increase per the model. |

> **Playbook Implication:** The 180-day rule is critical for timeline planning. For any renewal-focused playbook, calculate the 180-day cutoff date and flag it. After that date, the only option is standard renewal at the increased price.

## 4. Retention & Win-Back

### Retention Strategy

Primary retention lever: **extend contracts into 3-year or 5-year terms** to lock in price and reduce annual churn risk.

Secondary: Identify and resolve product pain points via professional services before renewal discussions begin. A customer who feels heard and supported is more likely to accept the pricing model.

### Win-Back Rules

If a customer leaves:

1. **Conduct a post-mortem analysis** — understand exactly why they left.
2. **Send a well-crafted win-back email** — acknowledge the situation, do not be defensive.
3. **Possible win-back offers (REQUIRE CEO APPROVAL):**

| Win-Back Offer | Conditions | Approval Required |
|---------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Flat renewal** (no price increase) | Customer left due to pricing concerns. Must sign 3-year or 5-year contract. | CEO approval |
| **Free Platinum upgrade** | Offered to incentivize return. Must sign 3-year or 5-year contract. | CEO approval |

**Purpose of win-back offers:** Secure the customer for an additional 1-3+ years to either (a) lock in cash or (b) buy time to improve customer sentiment around the product.

> **Playbook Implication:** Win-back scenarios should always include the approval requirement. Never present flat renewals or free Platinum as options Jay can offer independently. Model these as "escalation paths requiring CEO sign-off."

## 6. Communication & Disclosure Rules

### What We Say vs. What We Don't

| Topic | Standard Communication | What We Don't Disclose |
|-------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Pricing** | "Our pricing is standardized and non-negotiable, driven by our continued investment in product stability and performance." | That win-back offers (flat renewal, free Platinum) exist as CEO-approved exceptions. |
| **Contract terms** | "Our contract is a standardized, non-negotiable document as part of our company policy." | That >$100K customers can request redlining. |
| **Downsells** | "Reducing licenses will not affect your contract price." | — (this is stated directly) |
| **Multi-year** | "Signing a 3-year or 5-year contract locks in your current price and protects you from annual increases." | There are no hidden discounts or additional incentives beyond price protection. |

> **Playbook Implication:** Every playbook must include a "Messaging Guidelines" section that specifies exactly what can and cannot be said for that customer's situation. Proactive disclosure violations undermine negotiating position.

## 7. Decision Tree — What Can I Offer?

```
Customer Renewal Scenario
├── Active Customer (>180 days to renewal)
│   ├── Offer multi-year extension (3 or 5 year) at CURRENT price
│   ├── Offer professional services for pain point resolution
│   ├── Upsell additional licenses (prorated)
│   └── Upsell Standard → Platinum (if value proposition is strong)
│
├── Active Customer (≤180 days to renewal)
│   ├── Standard renewal at 25% or 45% increase
│   ├── Offer professional services
│   └── Upsell additional licenses (prorated)
│
├── Customer Pushing Back on Price
│   ├── Reframe: show compounding cost of annual renewals vs. multi-year lock-in
│   ├── Do NOT offer discounts
│   ├── Do NOT offer flat renewal (unless win-back scenario with CEO approval)
│   └── If >$100K and they push on contract terms: respond to redline request (do not initiate)
│
├── Customer Threatening to Leave
│   ├── Identify root cause (price? product? support? champion loss?)
│   ├── If product issues → professional services engagement
│   ├── If support issues → escalate internally, demonstrate improvement
│   ├── If price → model multi-year savings, emphasize investment rationale
│   └── Do NOT preemptively offer concessions outside the model
│
└── Customer Has Left (Win-Back)
    ├── Conduct post-mortem
    ├── Send win-back email
    ├── If pricing was the issue → escalate to CEO for flat renewal or free Platinum on 3-5yr term
    └── All win-back offers require CEO approval
```

## 8. Rules for Playbook Generation

When generating any customer-specific playbook, the following checks must pass:

1. **Pricing compliance** — Every pricing scenario must use exactly 25% (Standard) or 45% (Platinum). No other figures.
2. **Contract compliance** — Only 1-year, 3-year, or 5-year terms. No custom durations.
3. **No unauthorized concessions** — Flat renewals, discounts, or free upgrades only appear in win-back scenarios clearly marked as requiring CEO approval.
4. **180-day rule** — If the customer is ≤180 days from renewal, multi-year extension is not available. Flag this in the playbook.
5. **Redline rule** — Contract modification only mentioned as a reactive option for >$100K customers, never as a proactive offer.
6. **Downsell awareness** — If a customer is considering reducing scope, note that price does not decrease with license reductions.
7. **Disclosure compliance** — Messaging guidelines must respect what we do and do not proactively disclose.
8. **Margin awareness** — PS recommendations should be positioned as retention tools protecting software revenue, not standalone margin plays.
9. **Compounding math** — Always show the customer their price trajectory under annual renewals vs. multi-year lock-in.
10. **Approval flags** — Any recommendation requiring CEO or legal approval must be clearly flagged with `⚠️ REQUIRES APPROVAL` markers.
